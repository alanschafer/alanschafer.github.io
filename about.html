About Me

Hi, I'm Alan Schäfer. At Nexus Analytics, I explore the intersection of belief systems and psychological experience - examining how deeply held convictions shape our reality, particularly in moments of crisis, trauma, and personal transformation.

The Research

My focus centers on belief deconstruction - the process of questioning and dismantling long-held religious or ideological frameworks. This often occurs following significant life experiences, including trauma, that challenge previously unexamined assumptions about reality, meaning, and truth.

Through analyzing biblical texts, exploring cognitive mechanisms of belief formation, and examining the psychological impact of systematic control, this research offers pathways for independent thought and intellectual freedom.

The Approach

This work emerges from personal inquiry but is rigorously refined through logical analysis and held to scholarly standards. Each investigation applies critical thinking, evidence-based reasoning, and systematic examination of sources to ensure intellectual integrity.

While the questions arise from lived experience, the methodology emphasizes careful research, logical consistency, and transparent reasoning. The goal is honest exploration that meets academic rigor - creating reliable insights for others working through similar questions about belief, trauma, and independent thought.
